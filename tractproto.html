Jesus, the Missing Piece

THE UNIVERSE

Many people think that the universe just happened, that the big bang just occurred for no reason; that humans were created simply by random processes of evolution.

GOD CREATED IT

This is not true. God created the world1 through his son, Jesus Christ.2 Jesus is the origin, the sustainer and also the final purpose of all the pieces of the universe. Because of Jesus, the universe is not random or chaotic, but obeys mathematical laws. Finely tuned. Humans came into existence on a planet of life - because God planned it.

GOD HIDES

The bible - the book written by humans, but inspired by God - says that God is a “God who hides himself”3. Nevertheless he was and is always there working often invisibly behind the scenes through his son, Jesus Christ4.

ADAM AND EVE PUNISHED

Because of their disobedience, the first humans Adam and Eve were cast out of paradise5 and punished with death6. All their descendants inherited a predisposition to sin from them7, and became destined for eternal death8, 9.

MESSIAH PROMISED

God wanted to rescue humankind from these consequences10. He promised he would one day send a special person, a mysterious “messiah” to rescue mankind11. He said it was important that people trusted in and obeyed the messiah when he came12.

MESSIAH A MYSTERY

Through his prophets, he told people in advance what the signs would be so that everyone could recognise him13, and even at what time he would come14. But many things were still hidden, a “mystery”15. The prophets and even angels in heaven thought hard about this, trying to decode God’s clues16.

BORN IN BETHLEHEM

One clue was that the messiah would be born in Bethlehem in Israel17. And then when the messiah was about to be born, 2000 years ago, God sent a special star to lead the way to him18. Wise men from the East followed the star and found the young Messiah, Jesus Christ, in Bethlehem19.

GOD WITH US

Yes, Jesus had become a human being of flesh and blood20, God with us. The invisible “missing piece” had suddenly become visible at a particular time in history21, 22.

MIRACLES

When the Messiah grew up, he started doing miracles, which were further signs to prove to people who he was23.

HATED BY RELIGIOUS LEADERS

Some people recognised that he was the one we must follow24, but others - in particular the religious leaders of the time - did not recognise that he was the Messiah25, 26 or want to follow him27, but rather they wanted to kill him28. They didn’t recognise him because the Devil had blinded their eyes to the truth29.

JESUS CRUCIFIED

Eventually they did kill him30. They arranged for the Romans, who ruled the world at that time, to nail him to a cross of wood, murdering him in the most painful manner31. But this did not catch God out unawares. God had already planned that this would happen32 so that he could forgive humans33 and enable them to come back to a friendship with Him again34.

RESURRECTION

Jesus was then resurrected from the dead35, which was a sign that humans would eventually be resurrected too36.

GREAT COMMISSION

He instructed his followers (“disciples”) to tell others about him and persuade them to follow him too37…

SAUL CONVERTED

One of the religious leaders, called Saul, hated the Christians and went about imprisoning and killing them38. One day a blinding vision of Jesus knocked him down. The vision asked Saul, “Why are you persecuting me?” But Saul didn’t recognise who it was until the vision revealed that he was Jesus39. The final piece of the puzzle fell into place for Saul, and he became a follower of Jesus too40.

IDOLS IN ATHENS

One of the places where Saul tried to make disciples was Athens in Greece41. They had lots of temples there and lots of different idols - gods of stone and metal and wood that they bowed down to42. But they did not know about Jesus - the only one truly worthy of worship.

AN UNKNOWN GOD

However, there was one altar that Paul noticed, which had written on it “To an unknown God”43. Maybe the Athenians had a feeling that there was a mysterious God behind the scenes that they didn’t yet know about but that nevertheless required their worship?

IT’S JESUS!

Saul said “The one you worship as unknown, I now declare to you.” And he told them all about Jesus.

MEN MUST REPENT

Saul explains: “Although God overlooked the ignorance of earlier times, He now commands all people everywhere to repent.”44 In other words, men must abandon the bad things they are doing and the other things they are worshiping, and trust in Jesus to rescue them45, obeying him as their Lord46.

HEAVEN OR HELL?

This is so important as otherwise they are destined for judgment and eternal torment in hell47, 48. For those who believe, there is eternal life awaiting and joy with Jesus in heaven49, 50.

IDOLS TODAY

So what about you? Look around at the idols where you are - temples and statues but also shopping centres and sports arenas? Family and romantic relationships? Personal beauty? Business success? God demands that all things must take second place to Jesus. Will you turn away from worshiping other things and make Jesus number one in your life? If you want to do this, then pray the following prayer with your whole heart and Jesus will accept you.

TURNING TO JESUS

“Dear Jesus, I am sorry for the bad things I have done in my life and the other things I have put before you. Please forgive me. I turn away from these things and put my trust in you, making you Lord of my life. Thank you for your forgiveness, love and acceptance, which you promise to all who come humbly to you.” Did you pray the prayer? Do you want to find out more about Jesus and what Christians believe?
